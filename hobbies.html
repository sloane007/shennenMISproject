Hobbies

1. Track and Field

Since my freshman year of high school, I have been a thrower in track and field. I competed in both shot put and discus for those 4 years on varsity. When I came to UMD this year, I stayed committed to the sport and joined the women’s track team. Since being here, I have begun to learn about and how to do the additional throwing events, including hammer. Due to this hobby, I have also become dedicated to lifting to get stronger. Since the beginning of 2025, I really got into lifting and have been consistently lifting 3 times a week since then. It doesn’t matter if I am actively training with the team or lifting by myself during the summer, I have grown much fonder of the activity than I once was.

2. Baking

One of my favorite things to do when I am bored or just need a way to relax is to bake. Some things that I love to make are bread (sourdough, focaccia, or even just a plain white loaf), cookies, and cakes. If I ever want to do something for someone, my default is to spend some time in the kitchen baking them something that I know they like.

3. SCTA (Scott Carver Threshers Association)

One of the hobbies that I am most proud of is the amount of volunteering I do, especially at the Scott Carver Threshers in Jordan, MN. I have been a member of this non-profit since 2020. In 2023, I took on the role of head of the barn and livestock committee. Additionally, that year I was voted onto the board, which made me the youngest female board member in the club’s history. But regardless of those achievements, I have spent nearly 200 hours within the last 2 years working on repairing the old barnyard that had been only used for storage for over a decade. I recognized many safety hazards that would affect both animals and visitors. Addressing these issues was crucial to do before the barnyard could be reopened. In addition to this, I made many changes to the design of how things worked in order to make processes easier and more efficient. But, before I started any of this work, I had to get approval from the membership and a budget of $1000 to be granted. In August 2024, the barnyard was reopened for the first time in over 10 years for the 60th annual SCTA show, and it was a huge success! In August 2025, the same thing was repeated, but with even more animals! This hobby of mine has led to a lot of growth and development in me as a person. The last thing to add is that in October 2024, I was elected to be the club’s Secretary. Volunteering at STCA has helped me grow in are my problem-solving abilities, public speaking skills, as well as listening and note-taking skills.

4. Being Outside

Being outside is something that I love, no matter the season. In the winter, I ski with my family at places around Minnesota and occasionally in Colorado. In the spring, I am outside all the time due to track practice and meets. In the summer, I spend lots of time at the lake, parks, or even in the garden/yard. Finally, in the fall, I love to go for walks on trails and look at the surrounding landscapes.

5. Barrel Racing

Though it is not an active hobby of mine at this moment, barrel racing will always be something that I have a passion for and plan to get back into. I think that it is just as important to have on this list as some of the other things for this reason. I grew up around horses and competing on them. I stopped regularly riding around 2022 when my 2 horses were put into partial retirement. Since then, I occasionally get to spend time with friends and ride, but I have not competed in over 3 years. Once I get the chance in the future and have the financial capacity to take on this commitment again, I plan to do so, and it is something that motivates me every day.
